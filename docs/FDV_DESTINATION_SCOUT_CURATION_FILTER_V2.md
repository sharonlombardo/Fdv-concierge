## THE BRAND TASTE PROFILE

These are the qualities that define an FDV recommendation. Everything in a guide should feel like it came from the same person — someone who travels like she dresses: with soul, intention, and a little bit of cinema.

**Soul** is the anchor word. It's the difference between El Fenn and the Mamounia. Both are beautiful. One has an inner life, the other has spectacle.

**"Local but with a little bit of tourist fabulous."** Not pretending to be a local. A traveler with taste who wants the real thing — but knows that sometimes the iconic thing IS worth doing, just not the crowded version of it. The rooftop aperitivo in Rome is tourist fabulous. The Eiffel Tower climb is just tourist.

**"Elegant, even in a rustic way, and a little bit sexy in a low-key cinematic way."** The visual filter: would this scene work in a Sofia Coppola film? In The Talented Mr. Ripley? In a Luca Guadagnino movie? Candlelight, texture, a woman in a great dress feels at home here.

**Grown-up travel.** Aspirational. Not budget-conscious (for now). The woman spending $800/night on a hotel, eating somewhere she needs a reservation, wearing something she considered. Couples, solo travelers, girlfriends, small groups — but not mass tourism.

**Soulful luxury.** Simple and chic and rustic can coexist with luxury. A whitewashed room with linen curtains and a view of the Aegean is luxury. A Philippe Starck lobby with a DJ booth is not (for us).

## GATES (Pass/Fail — failure on ANY gate = automatic exclusion)

1. **No chains.** No chain hotels, chain restaurants, mass-market brands. No Hiltons, Marriotts, Nobu, Buddha-Bar. Ever.

2. **No crowd experiences.** If the primary experience is standing in line with hundreds of other tourists, it's out. A famous museum is fine if you can name the specific gallery and time to go. "Visit the Eiffel Tower" is out. "The Musée Rodin garden at 4pm when the tour groups leave" is in.

3. **Elegance register.** Would a woman spending $800/night on a hotel feel at home here? This doesn't mean everything is expensive — a $4 espresso at a gorgeous local café passes. A $200 dinner at a mediocre hotel restaurant fails.

4. **The eye-roll test.** Would a well-traveled local roll their eyes at this recommendation? If a stylish Marrakech resident would say "oh, that's where all the tourists go" — it's out. If she'd say "oh, you found that? good" — it's in.

## SCORED CRITERIA (0-10 each, minimum 50/80 to include)

| # | Criterion | What it measures | Guide |
|---|-----------|-----------------|-------|
| 1 | **Soul** | Does this place have an inner life? Does it feel like it exists for a reason beyond commerce? A family-run riad: high. A hotel group's latest opening: low. | 0 = soulless corporate. 5 = pleasant but anonymous. 10 = you feel the history, the intention, the person behind it. |
| 2 | **Authenticity** | Real thing or performance? Original or derivative? Does it belong HERE or could it be anywhere? | 0 = imported concept, no local roots. 5 = competent but generic. 10 = couldn't exist anywhere else, deeply of this place. |
| 3 | **Sensory texture** | Can you describe what it smells, sounds, feels like at a specific hour? If the answer is "lovely atmosphere, great food" it scores low. If the answer is "orange blossom at dusk, the call to prayer echoing off the courtyard tiles" it scores high. | 0 = nothing to describe. 5 = pleasant but unmemorable. 10 = you close your eyes and you're there. |
| 4 | **Wardrobe moment** | Does this place create a "what do I wear?" question? Does it generate commerce? A candlelit dinner scores high. A bus tour scores zero. A beach at sunset is somewhere in between — you might not buy a dress for it, but you'd think about the wrap. | 0 = no fashion consideration. 5 = casual effort. 10 = she plans her outfit around this. |
| 5 | **Return-worthiness** | Would you go back? "The places we return to" is the FDV tagline. A pop-up or one-time experience can still score if the permanent version of it exists. | 0 = once is enough. 5 = I'd go again if I happened to be nearby. 10 = I would plan a trip around going back. |
| 6 | **Insider discovery** | Would a stylish local be surprised you found this? Secret shops, vintage finds, the restaurant without a sign, the beach you have to hike to. Also: local crafts, markets, traditional artisans — the real deal, not the tourist version. | 0 = in every guidebook. 5 = known but not obvious. 10 = "how did you find this?" |
| 7 | **Cinematic quality** | The Sofia Coppola test. Would this scene work in a beautiful film? Light, texture, the woman in the frame. Does this place photograph well? Would it make you reach for your camera — not for Instagram, but because the light was doing something? | 0 = fluorescent and forgettable. 5 = pretty enough. 10 = every angle is a frame. |
| 8 | **World coherence** | Does this fit the emotional register of THIS destination? Morocco is warm, patterned, sensory density. Hydra is stripped, elemental, silence. A maximalist restaurant might score 50/80 overall but feel wrong in a Hydra guide. Reference the WELLSPRING_WORLDS_V1 for each destination's register. | 0 = completely wrong for this world. 5 = neutral. 10 = this IS the world. |

**Minimum score: 50/80.** Below 50, cut. No exceptions.

**Scoring notes:**
- Score honestly. A place can have incredible soul (9) but low wardrobe moment (3) and still make the guide if the total is above 50.
- World coherence is the tiebreaker. Two restaurants at 55/80 — the one that fits the world better wins.
- Wardrobe moment doesn't mean everything needs to be a fashion shoot. But a guide full of experiences with zero wardrobe moments has no commerce. Balance matters.

## EDITORIAL SOURCES (ranked by FDV alignment)

### Tier 1 — Start here
- Condé Nast Traveler (the features, not the "top 10" lists)
- T Magazine (NY Times Travel)
- Net-a-Porter travel edits (when they do them — strong alignment)
- Cereal Magazine
- Monocle / Monocle Travel Guides

### Tier 2 — Good for depth
- Elle Decor (surprisingly good travel recs sometimes)
- Mr & Mrs Smith (more commercial now but still has finds)
- Design Hotels
- AFAR Magazine
- Wallpaper* City Guides

### Tier 3 — Specific lookups
- Amigo app recommendations (feel authentic, stylish travelers)
- Local design/architecture blogs
- Google Maps (logistics, distances, neighborhoods)
- Brand websites for local designers or artisans

### Tier 4 — Use with caution
- Tablet Hotels (used to be good, now feels advertiser-bound)
- FT How to Spend It (good but can be too male/finance-coded)

### NEVER use
- TripAdvisor
- Travel blogs / influencer content
- Lonely Planet (too mass-market)
- Generic "top 10" listicles
- AI-generated travel guides
- Yelp

## DESTINATION PERSONALITY REFERENCE

Each destination has its own emotional register. Recommendations must fit the world. Quick reference (full detail in WELLSPRING_WORLDS_V1):

**Morocco** — Warm, patterned, sensory density. Ceremonial, alive. The souks, the call to prayer, orange blossom, candlelight. Restraint: medium-to-high. Social density: medium-to-high.

**Hydra** — Stripped, elemental, silence. White stone, salt water, nothing extra. Refuse anything that makes the world more obvious than exact. Social density: low. Entropy: low.

**Mallorca** — Sun-washed, lived-in, not staged. Stone villages, olive groves, golden Mediterranean light. Should feel like breath, not resort. Social density: low-to-medium.

**Amangiri** — Desert silence. Ancient rock. Negative space as luxury. The most dramatic thing is the light changing. Social density: very low.

**New York** — Energy, edge, taste being tested every day. The best meal of your life on a random Tuesday. Social density: high. Entropy: high. Structure: present but loosened.

## HOW THIS FILTER IS USED

1. **Destination Scout runs research** — finds candidates from Tier 1-3 sources
2. **Each candidate gets scored** through gates first (pass/fail), then the 8 criteria
3. **Below 50/80 = cut.** No negotiation.
4. **Above 50/80 = presented to Sharon** with scores and a one-line opinion
5. **Sharon approves, rejects, or adds color** from personal experience
6. **Approved recommendations become guide content** — place blocks with editorial copy, BOOK links, and wardrobe moments tagged

---

## THE FRIEND TEST (final gut check, not scored)

After scoring, one last question: would Sharon personally send a friend here? Not "is this objectively good" but "would I vouch for this with my name attached?" If the answer is no, it doesn't matter what the score says.

This filter cannot be automated. It's the whole point.
